My program is a puzzle game. I wrote this game in scratch using the Scratch development environment. The purpose of my game is to make the player think and use their problem-solving skills to collect keys to open doors and progress through the game. The video shows an entire playthrough of all most mechanics. It demonstrates an inventory system that keeps track of the player’s items collected. I used MIT’s App Inventor to create my application. The app, “Class Time”, displays how much time before a class starts and how much time before a class ends. It also includes a feature to change the schedule from regular schedule to late start. When the application is launched, 3 buttons are presented. "Start" sends you to a screen where you can choose the period that is next and it will let you know how long until it starts, "End" send you to a screen where you can choose the period that you are in and it will tell you how long you are expected to be in that period. "Schedule" gives you a full and complete list of every schedule that is used throughout the entire school year; giving you a neat place to store that information.

The malware I chose to present is the ILOVEYOU worm. It found its way on victims computers by email. It would pretend to be some type of file that would trick the user to download it. It also faked its extension so the user would feel a false sense of security when clicking on it. Once activated, the worm changed registry settings and sent itself through your contact's linked emails to further spread. It caused damage by randomly overwriting the owner's files, ultimately causing over 15 billion dollars in damage in total. Making it one the most costly computer viruses.
